## 8. Responsible AI & Governance

### Core Principle

> **"The AI recommends. The recruiter decides."**

This system is a **decision-support tool**, not an autonomous hiring decision-maker. All final hiring decisions remain with qualified human recruiters.

### Protected Characteristics — Not Used

The AI does **not** infer, use, or factor in:

| Protected Characteristic | Status |
|------------------------|--------|
| Gender | ❌ Not used |
| Race or ethnicity | ❌ Not used |
| Religion | ❌ Not used |
| Political views | ❌ Not used |
| Health status | ❌ Not used |
| Age | ❌ Not used (years of experience used only as role-relevant proxy) |
| Family status | ❌ Not used |
| Nationality | ❌ Not used (language fluency assessed only when explicitly required by role) |

### Governance Framework

1. **Explainability:** Every recommendation includes evidence from the CV and a score breakdown by dimension
2. **Auditability:** All scoring weights and criteria are documented and transparent
3. **Human Override:** Recruiters can override any AI recommendation with documented reasoning
4. **Bias Monitoring:** Regular audits should compare AI recommendations against hiring outcomes across demographic groups
5. **Feedback Loop:** Recruiter decisions (accept/reject AI recommendation) should be tracked to improve model accuracy
6. **Data Privacy:** All candidate data processed in compliance with GDPR requirements
7. **Escalation Path:** Edge cases and borderline candidates flagged for human review ("Need More Info" category)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Algorithmic bias | Transparent scoring criteria; no protected characteristics used |
| Over-reliance on AI | "Hold" and "Need More Info" categories force human judgment |
| False negatives | Cross-role matching ensures candidates aren't prematurely rejected |
| Data quality issues | 5 CVs flagged as unreadable (image-based) — manual review required |
| Score inflation/deflation | Regular calibration against recruiter assessments |
